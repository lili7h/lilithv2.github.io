## Fennecs, Firearms and Fairybread

### Chapter 1: Raid at the '7th Ring'

The armoured truck jolted to the side as it carried its weight down the narrow, bustling streets of South Harbour Sydney. The setting sun obscured behind the south-harbor megastructures that dot this side of the city, casting rusty rays of light and shadow to pour through the truck's armoured windows. Inside the track rustled five Heavy Arms Tactical Response operators, armed to the teeth and sporting full tactical exo-armour. HATR squads were the Australian Federal Government's response to the ever increasing threat of super-powered organised crime, in the form of the Australian Sovereign Enforcement Agency, colloquially referred to as ‘Ace’. While the squad members themselves were not super-powered (for one, if they were, they’d be getting paid a lot more), they were highly-trained and highly-equipped elite soldiers utilised to counter organised crime and terrorism, where no or lower-class superpowers were involved. This usually meant if there was even a hint of Korps involvement, the ASEA would be sent home and the Hero Assembly called in instead - something that had been happening with more regularity in the recent months. A frustrating reality - getting geared up for a mission, adrenaline dumping into the bloodstream, fur bristling, only to have HeroDiv rock up, take jurisdiction and send them all packing.
{: style="text-align: justify;" }

Dale habitually checked his armour, running sensor diagnostics and flicking through the various HUDs and imaging cameras mounted to the helmet. The Horned Wolf had somehow found himself in charge of this particular Australian Sovereign Enforcement Agency squadron, failing upwards as he would humbly claim. He fidgeted with one of his horns as he waited for the suit diagnostics to complete, the onyx protrusions curling down behind his pointed canine ears and forward, tips framing his muzzle. The closeness of the horns perturbing the tufts of fur that adorned the flares of his jaw. The horns were a parting gift from his vacant mother, disappearing shortly after his birth, as creatures of the spirit and demon worlds tend to do. Though specifically what kind of spirit she was? Dale did not know - his father never talked about her, and he never asked.
{: style="text-align: justify;" }

The towering bull to his left eyed him with a smirk, “Thinking of goring the poor cunt, are ya’ Luck?”, gesturing broadly to Dale’s horn-bound ministrations. 

Brax Peyton - or ‘Dunk’ as the squad favoured him - was Dale's informal second in command, and the Squads primary breacher. ‘Luck’ was Dales’ own nickname, earned for himself after too many times coming out of a firefight without a scratch, despite lead rain landing multiple members of his squad in the infirmary. 
{: style="text-align: justify;" }

The ‘poor cunt’ in this case, was the reason they’d all piled into this truck and sped out of the barracks - ASEA intelligence had indicated that notorious arms dealer and wanted criminal ‘Thrash’ was planning to negotiate a deal in the “7th Ring” nightclub with local organised crime gang “Kix’s Crew”. The 7th Ring was a known hotspot for criminal goings-on, located in a particularly densely populated part of South-harbour locally known as ‘The pillars’. The ASEA had issued a warrant for the arrest of ‘Thrash’, though the historical record of high-profile criminals surviving an ASEA warrant was… not promising.
{: style="text-align: justify;" }

“Thinkin’ of pounding ya’ mum’s poor cunt” Luck retorted, earning a snort from the bull.

There was always banter before an op, crude boyish humour erring on the broad side of derogatory. Luck always entertained it, an ‘outlet for battle nerves’ he would describe it. Luck didn’t really enjoy it per se, but as he saw it, it was more important for the team to ‘bond’ than it was for him to have a pleasant conversation.   
{: style="text-align: justify;" }

“Soo like the horny dog, fantasising about Dunk’s mummy~” The armoured snow leopard sitting across from Luck joined in. 

Lyra, fondly aliased “Liar” by the team, was a dichotomy of flirtatious wit and puritan innocence. She was adamant about being a virgin (and proud of it too), something the team had decided on their own was definitely a lie. The snep had a fiendish tongue on her, which definitely did not align with the idea that she was ‘saving herself for the right cat’. 
{: style="text-align: justify;" }

“Ya’ know much about this horny dog, Lies?” Jaime smirked, the armed bobcat and squad medic raising his eyebrow at her, a paw flicking vaguely in Luck’s direction.

“My only claim to ‘fame’ is knowing too much about a particular horny lynx”, Lyra retorted, sarcasm evident.

“I aim to please!”

“Fiz, no one wants to know about your dicks”, Dunk deadpanned.

“Whaaaat? That's nonsense, truly” Fiz defended, casting a sideways glance at Lyra, unashamed by his horny Lynx shenanigans.

The banter was always like this, either the most inane vaguely horny conversation you could entirely ignore and not miss anything important, or it was the next hottest talking point in anti-Korps propaganda. Last mission, Fiz and Dunk had spent 10 minutes animatedly discussing how best to ‘deprogram’ the ‘braindead queer cunts’. The entire thing had left Luck feeling quite sour, though he was not entirely sure why. Luck had stopped paying attention towards the end, noting only that the conversation only ended up somewhere along the lines of ‘they should be put to work in the redlight’. 
{: style="text-align: justify;" }

Luck had hated that. 

Seething at the idea that the thing of value in the queer supervillains was apparently the shapes of their bodies - according to his team. His team. They think like this because he failed to school them. Because his leadership wasn’t good enough. He ought to have jumped in at that moment, cut them short. Perhaps he could have played it cool? *Cut the chatter, stay frosty*. He could get away with that. He’d heard that one in *Call of Duty: Werewolves*. Maybe ‘*mind on mission, quit ya bitchin*’ would have worked? He definitely wouldn’t have told them the real reason he wanted them to shut up. That would only earn him the target.
{: style="text-align: justify;" }

“5 minutes out.” Kiran interrupted his inner monologue, the strictly-business jackalope catching Luck's gaze with a nod. 

“Thanks Keys. Alright squad - final checks. Weapons warm, coils singing” 

The banter died as the squad made ready. Luck and Fiz prepare their Gaussrifles, coils ‘singing’ as they enter mission-ready status, coils becoming energised with a truly staggering current. The haunting song that prefaced every combat mission the squad had ever fought, and promised their safe return. A dangerously alluring dance that never failed to brighten an acidic day in down-town Sydney. There was a bitter-sweet excitement to a raid for Dale - a chance to truly stretch his legs, an opportunity to let the ruthless savageness of combat wash over him. Yet, there was also terror. Death was an optional result of combat, and no one liked death. It also spoke of distraction. When you’re on the mission, the mission is the only thing. Nothing else exists. His problems stay in the armoured truck, and he has a supersonic coilgun with superhardened ferrite slugs to deal with any new problems that show up. That part was always his special interest - the feel, the heft of the gun in his paws. The *feeling* as another slug is sent down range, the kickback into his shoulders. The ionic smell of the coils staining his sensitive nose - it was addicting.
{: style="text-align: justify;" }

Luck, for his own enjoyment more so than operational protocol, prayed that the Korps decided to stay home today.
